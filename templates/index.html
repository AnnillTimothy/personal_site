About Me

My love for software in grounded in my obsession for business and commerce.

In a society increasingly dependent on data, it fundamentally reshapes what we knew as value every century before.
A great product means nothing if it can't be scaled, and in an age driven by information and autonomy, software provides an all inclusive solution to better and bigger business. The more data, the more value. Saving credit card information to facilitate easier checkouts, targeted advertising from cached data to encite larger spending. It all made sense.

- and that is why I code.

I started to develop software because I loved business and finance and it's this unique distinction that sets me apart from every developer who's primary interest lies within writing code.
I see the clients needs directly and I see it first, which allows me to relate with their core needs and desires.
While the average developer creates a website, I enjoy integrating the system that requires the website in the first place.
A good looking website and a great product is truly worthless if there's no underlying system in place to scale it. With good software alone and SEO techniques, targeted advertising, calls to action, etc. most of these aspects can be achieved with minimal effort.

I'm a naturally curious and it enables me to learn quickly, so when a new technology comes along that makes a previous task easier, I'm all ears. I enjoy the process of creating something sustainable and impactful, things that make sense and will last the test of time.
Things like choosing niche specific colour palletes and designing industry specific logos that form the foundation of every company's existence, and figuring out how to structure and set up company specific data structures to most effectively manage and utilise customer data really brings me solace.
Connecting and integrating all of the intricate parts into one impressive software solution that make the entire business come together makes my world go round.
It's the thrill of seeing it all come together in production working as a singular system, exactly as I planned that keeps me wanting to do it again.
